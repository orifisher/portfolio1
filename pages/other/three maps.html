Shifting Utilizations of Urban Structures, NYC 1922 vs. 2016

The following maps demonstrate the shifting utilizations of NYC urban structures. The left-hand map shows NYC industrial areas circa 1922, encompassing industries such as textile, leather tanning, food and chemical industries, metal-works etc. The central map shows the current use of the same buildings, as residential, commercial, industrial and more. The right-hand map combines data from both aforementioned maps. It foregrounds the fact that areas which previously housed industries often involving hazardous materials, such as metal-works, chemical and lead-based paint industries, are now heavily populated, serving commercial and residential functions.

If you wish to change the area on view, click on any of the maps, and while holding down your left-hand mouse button, drag the marker towards your area of interest. The zoom can be changed via the +\- symbols on the left-hand map. Clicking on the color-coded areas of the map will open a window detailing the various structures' current use. To access any of the maps' legends, click on the "legends" link. [If the map halts, simply click "refresh"].
